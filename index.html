About Us

About Us

The Centre for Education and Sustainable Development in Africa (CESDA) is an International Network based in Kenya with regional chapters in Ghana, Nigeria, South Africa, Zambia and other African countries that host the Education for Sustainable Development (ESDA) and the Transforming Universities for a Changing Climate (Climate-U) projects.

  • Learn More>>>

Objectives

  1. Promote and contribute to theory and understanding of higher
    education and sustainable development.
  2. Support educational institutions' action on sustainable develop-
    ment initiatives.
  3. Support education institutions' contribution to climate action.
  4. Advance national and international collaborations in education
    for sustainable development.
  5. Promote universities' climate action and sustainable development
    through teaching, research, and community service.
  6. Build capacity and provide a platform for students and youth to take climate action and contribute to sustainable development.

Collaborations & Partnerships

Addressing sustainability problems requires concerted efforts by actors across all sectors. Equitable, socially inclusive and constructive multilateral systems are best suited to tackle societal challenges. Collective action is needed to form effective systems that align community development to a sustainability pathway. The collective action must extend beyond local, national and regional to global. CESDA’s goals on green education to solve sustainable development problems can only be met through long-lasting partnerships and collaborations with like-minded individuals and organisations. Our partners span across the globe and are drawn from multiple sectors, with each relationship focusing on a unique change to promote sustainable development.

We are working closely with 6 partners, the Sustainable Development Goals Center for Africa (SDGC/A), African Council for Distance Education (ACDE), African Network for Internationalisation of Education (ANIE), Association of African Universities (AAU), All-Africa Students Union (AASU) and GAIA Education Europe (GEE) to develop a Massive Open Online Course (MOOC). The MOOC strengthens national, regional and global policies that promote the development of courses and programmes on education for sustainable development and climate change education. This course presents an opportunity for students in African universities interested in comprehending the existential threats of climate change in their localities.

Green Education & Research

CESDA aims to support educational institutions in developing climate change education-oriented curriculums. Integrating climate change education into curriculums plays a salient role in training and equipping learners with knowledge, skills and attitudes necessary to address sustainable development challenges. Our research aims to impart learners, researchers, communities and policymakers with concerted practical knowledge and skills to combat sustainability issues.

Capacity Building

Capacity building is one of CESDA's core duties. Effective capacity building equips individuals, communities and institutions with knowledge, skills and values to respond to sustainability challenges. Our capacity-building programmes are founded on deep expertise and technical knowledge and comprehending the role of education, power, and politics in addressing change processes within complex societal systems. Effective capacity building requires working closely with our partners at multiple levels to promote sustainability agenda. Our capacity-building activities include:
i. Training learners and researchers on scientific expertise and leadership to advance education for sustainable development agenda.
ii. Community development by nurturing and supporting educational institutions-community partnerships to promote sustainable development.
iii. Disseminating climate information and other vital sustainability knowledge to the public through training.

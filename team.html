Team

Prof. Alice Odingo

Alice Atieno Oluoko-Odingo is an Associate Professor at the University of Nairobi, specializing in Geography, Population, and Environmental Studies. She has a Ph.D. in agricultural geography and is a founding member of the Maasai Mara Science and Development Initiative. Odingo has collaborated with UNEP on sustainable consumption policies and the IGAD Climate Prediction and Applications Centre on gender integration and disaster risk reduction. She has held influential positions, including Deputy Chairperson of the United Nations University and Board member of the global Association of Women's Rights in Development.

Dr. Jackline Nyerere

Dr. Jackline Nyerere, Senior Lecturer at Kenyatta University, focuses on educational leadership, climate change, and internationalization, focusing on rural and urban development, sustainable African society, and transnational research collaborations.She holds a PhD in educational leadership and policy from Kenyatta University, Kenya and professional training in Higher Education Leadership and Management from Carl Von Ossienzky University of Oldenburg, Germany.Jackline is currently a co-investigator in the Transforming Universities for a Changing Climate (Climate-U) project. She has served as co-chair of the Education for Sustainable Development in ANGR

Dr. Wilson Mutuma

Dr. Wilson Mutuma is a Lecturer in Educational Economics, Policy, and Planning at Kenyatta University, focusing on women's economic empowerment and climate change, and a member of KUQAN.He is a member of the Education for Sustainable Development in Africa Next Generation Researchers network as well as Kenya Universities Quality Assurance Network (KUQAN)Wilson is also a reviewer in a number of journals including, The Tuning Journal of Higher Education and The Kenya National Commission for UNESCO (KNATCOM). His research interest include education for sustainable development, quality assurance in higher education among other areas.

Dr. Purity Muthoni

Dr. Purity Muthoni is a lecturer in the Department of Environmental Planning and Management at Kenyatta University, Kenya. She holds a Ph.D. in Environmental Planning and Management. Purity is a researcher in the Transforming Universities for a Changing Climate project. She is a member of the Education for Sustainable Development in Africa Next Generation Researchers network. Her areas of expertise include environmental planning, Environmental Assessment/audit, governance and sustainability and climate change vulnerability and resilience.

Dr. Godwin Opinde

Dr. Godwin Opinde is a lecturer in the Department of Spatial & Environmental Planning at Kenyatta University. He holds a PhD in Environmental Planning and Management. Godwin’s areas of research interest include but not limited to climate change adaptation and mitigation, air quality management, transport planning, waste management, environmental governance and impact assessment. He is a researcher in the Transforming Universities for a Changing Climate project. A member of the Education for Sustainable Development in Africa Next Generation Researchers network. His has international & local experience in environmental matters having served in public service, civil society and academia.

Dr. Farai Kapfudzaruwa

Dr. Farai Kapfudzaruwa is a Research and Strategic Partnerships Manager at Future Africa, University of Pretoria. He has experience as a Grants Strategic Partnerships Specialist and a postdoctoral fellow at UNU-IAS, focusing on governance and linking the 2030 Agenda with national implementation strategies in Asia-Pacific and Africa. Dr. Kapfudzaruwa holds a Ph.D. in Business and Political Science from the University of Cape Town, South Africa, and has a focus on climate change governance in limited statehood areas like South Africa and Kenya.

Dr. Orleans Mfune

Dr. Orleans Mfune is a Senior Lecturer in the Department of Geography and Environmental Studies at the University of Zambia. He holds a PhD in Geography, a Postgraduate Diploma, Master of Human Ecology, and a Bachelor's degree in Geography. He has held positions as Head of the Department and Assistant Dean of the School of Natural Sciences. His research focuses on land resources governance, renewable energy resources, rural-urban linkages, and sustainable development.

Dr. Gerald Yiran

Dr. Gerald Yiran, an Associate Professor at the University of Ghana, holds Ph.D. , Master's, and BSc degrees in Geography and Resource Development. He is a member of various organizations and a fellow at the Centre for Urban Management Studies. His specializations include geographic information systems, vulnerability, climate variability, land degradation, sustainability, urban studies, and quantitative research methods.

Dr. Camillus Wongnaa

Camillus Abawiera Wongnaa holds a PhD. Agricultural Economics degree from the Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST), Kumasi, Ghana, an MPhil. Agricultural Economics degree from the University of Ghana, Legon, a BSc. Agriculture (Agricultural Economics option) degree from the KNUST and a Diploma in Education from the University of Education, Winneba, Ghana. He is currently a Senior Lecturer and an Agricultural Economist in the Department of Agricultural Economics, Agribusiness and Extension of KNUST. He is a member of Ghana Association of Agricultural Economists, African Association of Agricultural Economists, International Association of Agricultural Economists, Agricultural and Applied Economics Association and a Fellow of Africa Research and Impact Network.

Dr. Nashiru Sulemana

Dr. Nashiru Sulemana is a Senior Lecturer at the University for Development Studies in Ghana, with a Ph.D. in Rural Sociology and MSc in Agro-ecological Knowledge and Social Change. His research interests include sociology of rural and Agricultural Development, land acquisitions, smallholder agriculture, and sustainable development in Africa.

Dr. Olushola Fadairo

Dr. Olushola Fadairo is a Nigerian academic, researcher, and consultant with expertise in sustainable rural development, agricultural extension, project management, and policy issues. He specializes in process documentation of community development interventions, social inclusion, and sustainability indicators, and outcome mapping of rural and agricultural development projects. He has provided consultancy services to World Bank-assisted projects, USAID MARKETS II, and IITA on their Cassava and Maize Value Chain projects. Olushola is a member of the Agricultural Extension Society of Nigeria, Rural Sociological Association of Nigeria, and the International Society for Development and Sustainability.

Jeremy Kaburu

Jeremy Kaburu holds a Masters degree in Sustainable Urban Development from Kenyatta University. His research interest is in sustainable urban development, biodiversity conservation and environmental education. Jeremy's recent study was on the assessment of adaptive strategies to flood challenges in Mukuru slum in Nairobi City County, Kenya. He works as a Research Associate for the Transforming Universities for a Changing Climate research project in Kenya.
